The 11.2% Problem: Why Healthcare Boards Need Women Leaders Now

Women are the backbone of healthcare. We manage clinics, lead nursing teams, innovate in patient care, and drive outcomes. Yet we hold only 11.2% of CEO roles.

When it comes to the boardroom, we're barely in the room.

Board Composition Drives Results

This isn't ideology—it's economics. According to McKinsey, gender-diverse companies are 25% more likely to outperform financially. Yet healthcare boards remain overwhelmingly male and risk-averse, missing out on the innovation women leaders bring.

I've spent my career in healthcare transformation—from reimagining psychiatric care at Amen Clinics to leading Biote through its evolution as a public company. Every time a board embraced diverse perspectives, we made better decisions. Every time we played it safe, we lost ground.

The Real Problem Isn't Talent

Qualified women aren't overlooked for lack of credentials. They're overlooked for not fitting outdated pipelines.

Board recruitment lags behind the talent available. Executive search firms still operate on old networks. Board members still recruit in their own image. Meanwhile, exceptional women leaders are being passed over—not because they lack qualifications, but because they're invisible to traditional gatekeepers.

What Needs to Change Now

Healthcare faces unprecedented challenges: aging populations, rising costs, provider burnout, and the need to innovate faster than ever. These problems don't benefit from homogeneous thinking.

If you're a board member: Commit to interviewing at least one qualified woman for every open seat. Expand your search beyond traditional networks.

If you're a woman leader: Don't wait for an invitation. Connect with organizations like Women in Healthcare and Boardsi to expand your board opportunities. Your voice is needed.

The Path Forward

Real change requires boards willing to evolve and women willing to demand a seat at the table.

Let's make 11.2% history—and build a healthcare future that reflects the diverse workforce driving it.
